Raycast 2.0 turns your Mac launcher into a full workspace OS

The redesign folds AI commands, window management and a script store into one keystroke. We spent a week living in it — here's what actually sticks.

N

Nest Pany

Senior editor

Jun 25 2026·5 min read· 9.4 / 10

Raycast started life as a Spotlight replacement, a fast little launcher that power users kept in muscle memory. Version 2.0 is something bigger: an attempt to make the launcher the place where your whole workflow begins and, increasingly, ends.

After a week of forcing myself to reach for Raycast before anything else, the pitch mostly holds up. There are rough edges — and a pricing question we'll get to — but the core idea is sound, and occasionally it feels like magic.

What's new in 2.0

The headline change is AI Commands: natural-language actions you trigger inline without leaving the bar. Ask it to rename a batch of files, summarize the clipboard, or draft a reply and it routes through your model of choice.

  • Window management baked in — no more separate Rectangle install.
  • Script store with one-click install and per-command hotkeys.
  • Cloud sync for snippets, quicklinks and aliases across Macs.
For the first time, the launcher feels less like a search box and more like a command line you'd actually want to use.

Living with it for a week

The window management alone justified the switch for me. Half-screen snapping, throwing windows across displays, and restoring layouts after a dock/undock are all a keystroke away. It's not novel — but having it inside the launcher removes a whole category of context switching.

AI Commands are more hit-and-miss. When they work, they're a genuine accelerator. When the model stalls, you feel every millisecond, because the rest of Raycast is so fast.

The catch: pricing

The core app stays free. AI Commands and cloud sync sit behind Raycast Pro at $8/month. For heavy users that's easy math; for casual launcher fans it reframes a free tool as a subscription — and that will rub some people the wrong way.

Verdict

Raycast 2.0 is the rare update that earns the version bump. If you live on a Mac keyboard, it's an easy recommendation — just go in knowing the best parts are paid.
